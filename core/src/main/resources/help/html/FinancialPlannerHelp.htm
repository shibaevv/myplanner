FINANCIAL PLANNING SOFTWARE - version 1.0

Introduction

The Financial Planning software is designed to assist financial planners with the key planning process of generating financial plans.

allows the financial planner to capture client personal and financial information collected, undertake modelling of a variety of scenarios, produce projections, design strategies, and automatically generate a financial plan.

also enables financial planners and their support staff to store and update their full client database, for the purposes of ongoing maintenance. Data feeds of managed fund and ASX share information can be provided to automate updating of asset valuations. These datafeeds are subject to separate licences.

By following logical planning sequence you will find it easy to create high quality professional plans customised for your Dealer group.

Note on Data Collection

Some data is necessary to create a financial plan, some is collected for Client Relationship Management Purposes.

All information collected can be used in the plan writer, however not all fields are essential to creating a financial plan. For example, all contact details, including mobile phone numbers can be collected, however these are not generally required for a financial plan document.

Step 1. Data Collection - Input Clients Present Position

To create a reliable and accurate financial plan, you must have a good summary of your client's present position.

This includes identifying the client, understanding their risk profile, and understanding their assets, liabilities, income and expenses.

Entering Assets

You must enter your client's current assets. The software can cater for just about all asset types.

The standard categories of assets you may select are:

  • Cash Assets
  • Investment Assets
  • Physical / Personal Assets
  • Superannuation Funds
  • Income Streams

Every asset (excluding Personal and Physical Assets) require a name, a value, and income data to effectively complete your financial Assets and Liabilities report. Depending on the complexity of the plan you are creating you may add more detail for projections.

To create a simple assets and liabilities table you only need to enter the Asset Type, the Institution (or a comment), the Owner and the Current Value. However, if you wish to account for the income that the asset produces, any regular contributions or draw downs, entry fees etc you will need to enter additional information for the relevant asset.

The common data entry fields for each asset include:

Asset Type

-

A drop down list will be displayed for you to pick the type of asset. For example, if the asset is a cash asset, you may select if it is a savings account or a cash management trust.

Institution

-

This will determine the name that the asset is given in your reports. A drop down menu allows you to select a preset institution. If the institution is not available in the list you may enter a name in the Note and Comments field, and this will then be used as the Label for the asset in your financial reports.

Owner

-

You must select an owner for the asset. Owners of assets are reported in financial reports. Owners are required so that the income for tax analysis is reported to the correct taxpayer.

Current Value

-

You must enter a current dollar value for the asset. Some Assets (Managed funds and Shares) allow you to search for the asset name from the databases.

In these cases, a current unit price will be supplied and you may be required to enter the number of units. The current value will be determined by the software

Returns

-

You may be required to either enter a Total Return (cash Assets and Super Assets) or enter an income and growth return (Shares and Managed Funds). Returns must be entered as percentage values and are used by the software to provide future analysis, such as a wealth accumulation projections. Income percentages will be converted to dollar values for income analysis.

Annual Contributions

-

You can enter a dollar amount of annual contributions that are deposited into the asset by the client. Entering an Indexation percentage means that the annual contribution will increase by the Indexation percentage each year. You may also enter the period of time these contributions are to be deposited by entering the relevant dates in the start and end date fields.

Annual Draw downs

-

You can enter a dollar amount of annual draw downs that are withdrawn from the asset by the client each year. Entering an Indexation percentage means that the annual draw down will increase by the Indexation percentage each year. You may also enter the period of time these withdrawals are to be made by entering dates in the start and end date fields.

Up front Fee

-

You may enter a percentage for the entry fees. This percentage will be deducted from annual contributions.

Ongoing Fees

-

You may enter a percentage for ongoing fees. The value you put here should reflect the ongoing fees paid by the client for the investment. You can either in a total of all ongoing fees (management and your adviser fee). In this case you will need to ensure that the returns you input are gross returns so you do not double up on fees. Alternatively, you may enter only your adviser ongoing fees and input your return information as after management fees.

 

Cash Assets

You may enter your client's bank accounts or cash management trusts in the cash assets section. Generally, Cash Assets should be used for Savings accounts, Cheque accounts, Cash Management Trusts and Term Deposits.

Additional fields for Cash Assets include:

Maturity Date

-

This is an optional field and is for recording purposes only. It has no effect on the analysis models in this version.

Account Number

-

This is an optional field and is for recording purposes only.

Searching for Managed Funds and Shares using the Search Option

Managed Funds and Share information can be searched using the software databases which is derived from Morningstar and IRESS information. Note that the database contains both managed funds and shares within the same list.

To find the appropriate share or managed fund, you must select the &lsquo;Search' button and type the first few characters of the fund name in the description field.

For example, if you are searching for an AMP Managed Fund, you can just write AMP in the description box and the software will display a list of the AMP managed funds in the database. You can then select a managed fund by pressing the OPK button.

If you already know the funds investment code you can search by investment code in the second description box.

Shares can be quickly added by using the Investment code search option and entering the three-letter ASX code. E.g. to enter a Portfolio Shares as an asset, you only have to enter FPS in the second box, select FPS share and select OK. The current share price will be displayed in the financials.

You may also record any wrap / administration services your client holds. For example, if you are entering the details of a client with an existing Investment account, you may wish to enter the entire portfolio under the one heading &ldquo;Investment Service&rdquo; rather than record each underlying fund separately.

You may also wish to group your clients share portfolio into one heading titled &lsquo;Australian Share Portfolio rather than record each share individually. This decision will depend on your and the service you are offering your clients.

Step 2. Analysis of Clients Present Position

There are four key tools available to analyse and report on a client's present position. (Note that these same tools are available in Strategy Creator )

These are:

  • Wealth Analysis
  • Tax Analysis
  • Cash Flow Analysis
  • Asset Allocation

Wealth Analysis

The Wealth Analysis tool projects the value of your client's assets and liabilities for a future period of time (usually used to project capital until retirement). It is a combined view of your clients, partners and jointly held assets.

Wealth Analysis uses the information you have entered in the financials (or changed in strategy Creator) and projects forward each asset and liability. If you wish to change any of the earnings or other assumptions, you must change them in the financials screen (or create a new strategy).

There are three sections (tabs) in Wealth Analysis.

  • Assumptions - Allows you to enter further details before preparing your projections.
  • Wealth - Projects the accumulation of assets until the client's retirement date.
  • Retirement - Projects the capital over retirement years.

The wealth analysis tool should be used for wealth accumulators.

Assumptions

Before you can project a client's capital, you must first enter some basic assumptions as follows.

Years to Project

-

You should enter a total period of time in whole years which includes both the accumulation years and retirement years of the client. For example, if your client is age 50 and is retiring at age 60, you should select a figure of about 30 years to project till age 80.

 

Note, if you select a period of time less than the number of years to retirement you will not be able to project retirement capital.

Retirement Date

-

The retirement date is the date your client intends to retire. Note that you can only enter one date for couples. This date is automatically inserted if you have entered a retirement date in the client details section, however you can amend the date here if desired.

Retirement Income

-

You must enter a dollar value (in today's dollars) representing the income your client (combined income for couples) would like to receive during retirement. Based on the inflation percentage, the figure you enter is automatically adjusted to show the future value required.

Lump Sum required

-

You may enter a lump sum dollar amount representing a future expense to be made from the accumulated retirement capital. Clients may intend to purchase a new car, have a holiday or just plan to have a lump sum available at retirement over and above what is used to produce their retirement income. For example, if at retirement a client has $600,000, but requires a lump sum of $50,000, then $550,000 will be used to for projections.

Strategy after retirement

-

The earnings rates used for retirement capital is based on the seven broad investment strategies. You must select a strategy that matches your client's investment strategy objectives at retirement. The relevant income and growth percentages are displayed.

Repay Debt on retirement

-

Select this box if you want to automatically have any outstanding liability repaid at retirement. The funds will be subtracted from available funds, and the liability is removed from future projections.

Display

-

You must select how you want to display the data on screen and in your reports. The first year and the last year are always displayed. A maximum of 15 columns can be displayed in reports, therefore you should select a suitable display depending on the length of time until retirement.

 

Wealth View

Tax Analysis

The Tax Analysis estimates a full year tax position for the client and partner.

The tax report itemises:

  • Sources of Income
  • Tax Deductions
  • Tax Payable
  • Tax Offsets
  • Estimated Tax Payable and Net Income

It is displayed as a Word Document Report. You may edit this document, however values should only be edited in the financial data screens.

Cash Flow Analysis

Cash flow helps you analyse income and expenses of your clients. Projections can be made over a number of years. Instantly you can see what income surplus or shortfall your client has. This allows you to prepare financial strategies to either use the surplus income or assist with the shortfall.

All income and expense sources are itemised, including the estimated tax in the appropriate year. Also, once off expenses are visually displayed.

Assumptions

Many of the assumptions are already included and displayed if they have been entered in the Client Details or Wealth Analysis screens. However, you may need to enter or check the following assumptions.

Years to Project

-

You must select the number of years to project the cash flow forward.

Inflation

-

You must include an inflation percentage. Enter a percentage that is consistent with that used in other models.

Retirement Date

-

You must also enter the retirement date of the client.

Display

-

Select the setup for the columns you wish to display on screen and in your reports.

 

The Cash Flow Graph and table display the year by year income and expenses.

The last two rows of the cash flow table itemise:

  • The difference between income and expenses each year
  • The Potential Savings.

Potential savings is the accumulated surplus (or deficit) for that year. For example, if you clients has a cash flow surplus of $10,000 in year one and $11,000 in year two, the potential savings by year two is $21,000, representing the money that could potentially be saved or spent.

To edit the cash flow you must change the financial data. You may change all income and expense items in the financials screens.

Asset Allocation Analysis

The Asset Allocation tool lets you compare your clients existing asset allocation to that determined by the risk-profiling / ISO tool.

Editing the Asset Allocation

  • You must check all assets entered have an associated asset allocation. You can edit the percentage for each asset class by clicking a cell in the table. You select the cell appropriate to the Asset Name and Asset Class Column and change the percentage. Percentages must total 100% in the Total Column.
  • You can choose not to include an asset in the asset allocation analysis by deselecting the &lsquo;Include' check box for the appropriate asset in the Asset Allocation Table. This is often required to exclude a clients working cash account from the asset allocation.
  • By default all assets appear in the asset allocation table (excluding Personal Assets). If you want to show the clients own home in asset allocation you must make it an investment asset first in the financial screen.
  • Cash assets are automatically inserted as 100% Cash
  • Shares are automatically inserted as 100% Australian Shares
  • Managed Funds that have searched and selected using the Investment Search Function (searched from the Morningstar database) are automatically assigned the appropriate asset allocation.
  • You must manually enter the asset allocation for assets that are not linmked to Morningstar data, such as an employer super funds or wrap accounts.

 

Step 3 - Preparing Alternative Strategies

Once you have entered and analysed a client's information, you will be in a position to prepare some alternative financial strategies. Note that you may wish to explore the Calculators prior to this step.

You may perform the same financial analysis on the restructured financial data as you preformed on the original financial data.

Strategy Creator Overview

The Strategy Creator allows you to reallocate a client's investments to new investment assets. Also, you can change the client's income and expenditure, (and invest the surplus income).

You are presented with two tables in Strategy Creator for the "before and after" views of your clients position.

The top table is the clients existing Financials titled "Source of Funds". This data is the same information as you entered in the financials screen. To change this data, you should close the strategy creator and edit the information in the Client Data - Financials screen.

Allocated Pension Calculator

The Allocated Pension calculator allows you to prepare long term simulations of your clients allocated pension income stream.

In addition, you will be able to estimate the first year income payments (minimum and maximums) and deductible amounts.

To prepare an Allocated Pension simulation you must enter the following details:

Client Details

Client details includes basic client information. If you are using a client that has already been saved, some of the clients details may already be available in the Allocated Pension simulation. These can be adjusted if required.

Client Name

Enter the clients name as you would like it to appear on your reports.

DOB (date of Birth)

Enter the clients Date of Birth. This is essential for calculating the clients age, and their life expectancy, and minimum and maximum pension payments.

Pension Start Date

Enter the date you wish to commence the pension. Generally, pensions can commence at any time. Note you may enter a date in the past if you wish to project an existing pension.

Note, as with all date fields, you must enter dates as DD/MM/YYYY.

First Payment Date.

You may enter a date for the first actual payment. Allocated Pensions are paid on the 15th of each month. Generally, the first pension payment should be at least a couple of weeks after the Pension Start Date.

Sex

You must enter the gender of your client. This is essential for calculating the clients age, and their life expectancy, and minimum and maximum pension payments.

Age at Pension Start Date

This will automatically be calculated for you.

Eligible Service Period

You must enter an Eligible Service Period to ensure that the ETP components are calculated correctly. If you have not entered a date, you will be reminded when you try to enter the ETP details.

R/O from Pre 1994 Pension

You can check this box if the client's existing pension was established prior to July 1994. By default the selection is &lsquo;No'.

If the pension is to be rolled over in full to the new pension that you are simulating, then the Undeducted Purchase price (UPP) will differ to reflect the higher UPP for the pre 1994 pension.

ETP / Rollover Calculator

The ETP / Rollover calculator is used to determine the tax payable on the withdrawn components of an ETP.

The ETP calculator can also link to the allocated pension calculator should you select these options.

If you already have a saved client, some of the client details will be available in the ETP / Rollover Calculator. You can change these fields if required.

You may save and report multiple ETP Withdrawal options. These can be used in your plan document.

The following fields need to be completed to prepare a calculation.

DOB & Client Age

You must enter the client's date of birth.

If the client is over age 55, the option to add any used tax-free threshold will not be available.

He clients age is automatically calculated.

Calculation date

A calculation date (DD/MM/YYYY) must be entered so that the calculator can work out the correct Eligible Service Period and components as at the date of calculation. You can enter a future date if required.

Eligible Service Period

You must enter the Eligible Service start date of the ETP amount. The eligible service period is calculated based on the days between this date and the calculation date.

Tax Rate

This field allows you to enter an estimated marginal tax rate for the client. Assessable portions of income from the withdrawn ETP (e.g. 5% of the Pre 1983 component) will be taxed at the rate of tax you have entered.

For example, if you client is on the highest tax bracket (already earning over $60,000) per year, you can enter the highest rate of tax of 48.5%. (Including Medicare).

Post 30/6/1983 tax-free used

If your client is over age 55, a portion of the post June 1983 component can be withdrawn without incurring tax.

This tax-free amount reduces by any previously withdrawn tax-free amounts.

If your client has already used a portion of the tax-free threshold, you must enter that amount here. The calculator will then determine the remaining portion that is tax free.

By default the field is empty, assuming that the full post June tax-free threshold is available for your client.

Total ETP

Enter the total ETP amount.

Note: The calculator allows you to perform partial withdrawal calculations, therefore you may enter the total ETP amount.

The software will determine the portions of Pre and Post 1983 components.

Note: If the ETP consists of other components, such as undeducted contributions, you are able to enter these dollar amounts in the ETP Allocation input fields. The software will then correct the pre and post 1983 components.
